Who is Makoto Shinkai?

The Japanese manga artist, animator, screenwriter, director, and cinematographer is Makoto Shinkai. He typically works alone to develop and write the stories for his animes, as well as to direct and oversee the cinematography. In 1999, Shinkai published She and Her Cat, a five-minute animated video that served as his debut animation. Voices of a Distant Star (2002), The Place Promised in Our Early Days (2004), 5 Centimeters Per Second (2007), Children Who Chase Lost Voices (2011), and The Garden of Words (2013) are just a few of the mainstream anime films he would go on to produce. His biggest hit, Your Name, was released in 2016 and was rated as the top body-swap movie by IMDb. In the same year, Variety named him one of the "10 Animators to Watch in 2016." Your Name surpassed Spirited Away from Studio Ghibli to become the highest-grossing anime film of all time in 2017. Game Rant claims that Your Name is currently the second-highest-grossing anime film of all time as of May 2021. In 2019, Shinkai released Weathering With You, another popular feature animation. He received a 'Letter of Appreciation' from the National Policy Unit of the Cabinet Secretariat in 2012, identifying him as one of the "Global Messengers of Japan."


Early Life and Education

On February 9, 1973, Makoto Niitsu, also called Makoto Shinkai, was born in Koumi, Nagano Prefecture, Japan. Shinkai has been a cat lover since he was a young boy and has continued to raise cats throughout his life. "Ever since I was a child I always had cats with me. I'm from the Nagano prefecture, and while living there I had cats, and after I came to Tokyo I also picked up some strays and started raising them. So I definitely always had cats with me," according to him.


Beginning in middle school, Shinkai's love for drawing comics persisted throughout his time at the university. He attended Chuo University in Hachioji, Japan, to study Japanese literature. He drew children's picture books and was a member of the juvenile literature club at Chuo. Shinkai received his degree from Chuo University's Faculty of Letters in March 1996.


Early Career

Shinkai began working at the video game firm Falcom after graduating. He was employed by Falcom for five years as an artist, creating online designs and video clips for the company's games. His subsequent film career would be influenced by his stay at Falcom. At Falcom, he got to know Tenmon, a composer who would go on to score the majority of his movies.

Plot

Mitsuha Miyamizu, a high school student in Itomori, Japan, dreams of becoming a Tokyo boy. She intermittently switches bodies with Tokyo boy Taki Tachibana, living together and communicating through various means. Mitsuha sets Taki up on a date with Miki Okudera, while Taki helps Mitsuha become more popular. They visit a Shinto shrine, where Hitoha explains that God governs time and human connections. Taki goes on a date with Miki, but realizes he's falling for Mitsuha. The body-switching stops.


Taki, Miki, and Tsukasa travel to Hida to search for Mitsuha. They find Itomori, a village devastated by fragments from a comet. Taki realizes he and Mitsuha were separated by three years, living in 2013 and 2016. He loses his memories of Mitsuha and has a vision of her giving him a red ribbon. Taki awakens in Mitsuha's body, and Mitsuha's friends help him evacuate Itomori. They cross timelines, allowing them to meet in person for the first time.
